After completing the 10th grade, individuals have a range of options to consider for their future careers. These include pursuing diploma courses, enrolling in vocational training programs, participating in skill development initiatives, selecting career-oriented courses, or engaging in apprenticeships. Each of these pathways offers unique opportunities for gaining practical skills, industry-specific knowledge, and preparing for either immediate employment or further academic endeavors.

Science

Streams refer to specialized paths within science education, such as biology, chemistry, or physics, allowing students to focus on specific scientific disciplines.

Commerce

Streams are specialized areas of study within commerce or business education, such as finance, marketing, human resources, or accounting, enabling students to specialize in particular aspects of business management.

Arts

Within arts and humanities, streams denote concentrations within disciplines like literature, history, philosophy, or fine arts, allowing students to specialize in specific literary periods, artistic mediums, or historical eras.

Others

Streams exist in various fields, such as engineering disciplines (e.g., mechanical, electrical) or vocational training areas (e.g., automotive technology, culinary arts), providing individuals with opportunities for specialization and skill development within specific domains of study or profession.
